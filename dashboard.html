Designing a Dashboard App for Inaiways Customers that Reduced Customer Support Time by 95%

Dashboard App
Context & Business Problem

To execute AI-powered Google Ads for our clients, we initially ran ads through Inaiways’ Google Ads account on behalf of the clients. This posed a limitation, as we could not directly integrate the API into the clients’ Google Ads accounts. Consequently, providing campaign performance updates to clients required significant time from our customer support team.

The primary goals of the dashboard app were:

  • To provide clients with real-time insights into their campaigns.
  • To significantly reduce customer support time.

This app was developed after serving our first 5–10 clients and was a crucial step in scaling our operations rapidly.

Pain Points
  • Customer Support Team: Overwhelmed with status update meetings and constant client inquiries.
  • Clients (Based on Qualitative Analysis): Clients frequently needed to follow up for campaign updates, leading to frustration and inefficiency.
  • The increasing number of clients, combined with the limited capacity of our customer support team, created a bottleneck, necessitating an automated solution.
Research

To design the dashboard, I analyzed the most frequently asked questions from clients to the customer support team. The top queries were:

  • How much money has been spent so far?
  • When will the number of leads increase?
  • How many conversions have happened so far?
  • What should I do next for my business? (took this out of current scope)
  • Which products/services were in the highest demand.
  • Which regions showed the most searches for their offerings.
  • The most commonly searched user intent.
Ideation, Visual Identity & Data Representation
  • Dashboard Design: Since the data was an extension of Google Ads, I adapted Google’s brand colors for the dashboard’s visual identity.
  • Data Presentation: Research showed that marketers typically use bar graphs, pie charts, and tabular formats when reporting to CMOs and CEOs. These formats were incorporated into the dashboard.
Dashboard App
Impact of the Dashboard Implementation

Once the dashboard app was deployed, the customer success (CS) team handed it over to clients. The results were immediate:

  • Client support emails & calls reduced by 60%.
  • Common inquiries were now resolved via the dashboard.
  • Clients only contacted the CS team for major concerns or account recharge discussions.
Dashboard App
Next Iteration After 1.5 Months

70% Customers would still have calls with CS team to discuss impact that ads are bringing to their business

We realised our goal was not completely achieved as we had to make the data more informative for clients

Research

Customer success team would get questions like

  • How do I know the impact of my ads?
  • What kind of business has been generated from my ads?
  • The CTR is low and high but how do I know number of leads converted?

We started working on making the data more informative for clients by collaborating cross-functional, we understood how sales team was explaing the logic of ads spends to clients before they closed the deals and used the same logic to explain the data to clients.

Ideation - The funnel approach

After monitoring the ongoing customer interactions, we held a meeting with the CMO, CEO, and CS team. The CMO informed us about the technique they used - funnel-based approach to clearly demonstrate ROI.

This was a one of the breakthrough moment: We realized that providing a funnel view of ROI would bridge the gap in client understanding and highlight the impact of AI-powered ads.

Once we implemented the funnel visualization:

  • Customer support calls reduced by 95%.
  • Clients fully understood the impact of their ad spend.
  • Support calls were limited to discussions about next month’s ad budget.
Conclusion & Next Steps

The dashboard transformed our customer support workflow by minimizing the need for manual campaign updates. It enabled:

  • 95% reduction in support calls.
  • Greater client autonomy: Clients could independently track their ad performance.
  • Efficient scaling: Freed-up CS team bandwidth allowed us to onboard more clients seamlessly.
