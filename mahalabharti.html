Mahalabharti

Introduction

To enable citizens of Maharashtra to avail government schemes, especially helping poor people unaware of the schemes applicable to them, to improve their livelihoods.

PROTOTYPES - Before the Research, Based on Assumptions

Following the F pattern to show most important information

Important profile details were placed left, scheme benefits in the center, updates below, important notices top right, and user activity bottom right.

PERSONAS
ANALYSING RESEARCH

Performing Thematic Analysis

  1. People are interested in scheme benefits, not just scheme names.
  2. Internet and mobile network is scarce in villages.
  3. Awareness about schemes is very low.
  4. 95% villagers are unable to read/write.
  5. Commuting to banks, schools, offices is difficult.
  6. Corruption is prevalent in local govt. offices.
  7. Most villagers near poverty line need tech assistance.
IDEATION

Solutions Based on Research Insights

We proposed an offline + online system accessible via Panchayat computers with trained assistants helping citizens.

Citizens select schemes visually via pictures since literacy is low.

"Picture speaks a thousand words."

The assistant would register citizens and provide a printout of necessary forms and documents.

Flow of Service
  1. Citizen (or assistant) registers on the portal.
  2. Fills basic information.
  3. Selects role or scheme of interest.
  4. Completes additional details as required.
  5. Eligible schemes are shown.
  6. Receives printout of scheme documents.
INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE (IA)
PROTOTYPES - After Research

Application was created in Marathi, Hindi, and English with image-first design.

USABILITY TESTING

Guerrilla testing was conducted with citizens of various tech-savvy levels. Post usability improvements, task completion rates rose to 94%.

FEEDBACK and NEWS

Over 5,00,000 citizens registered and availed benefits. Mahalabharti was launched officially by the CM of Maharashtra and inspired other states like Haryana.

LEARNINGS

Talking to people in their native language helped break barriers and gather genuine feedback.
